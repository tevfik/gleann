# mRNA Vaccine Technology: Mechanisms and Design

## mRNA as a Therapeutic Platform

Messenger RNA (mRNA) vaccines deliver genetic instructions directly to cells, programming them to produce a target antigen. Unlike traditional vaccines using attenuated virus or protein subunits, mRNA vaccines require no cell culture growth, enabling rapid production.

The mRNA strand is typically 3,000–4,000 nucleotides long and is synthesized in vitro by T7 RNA polymerase from a DNA template. It encodes the antigen of interest flanked by 5' UTR and 3' UTR regulatory regions, a 5' cap structure, and a poly-A tail for stability.

## Lipid Nanoparticle Encapsulation

The primary challenge of mRNA delivery is protection from RNase degradation and cellular uptake. Lipid nanoparticle (LNP) encapsulation solved this challenge. LNPs are ~80–100nm diameter particles composed of four lipids: ionizable cationic lipid, phospholipid (DSPC), cholesterol, and PEG-lipid.

The ionizable lipid (MC3, SM-102, ALC-0315) carries neutral charge at physiological pH but becomes positively charged in the endosome, facilitating endosomal escape and cytoplasmic mRNA release. The PEG-lipid coating extends circulation time by preventing opsonization.

## Pseudouridine Modification and Innate Immune Evasion

Unmodified mRNA triggers strong innate immune responses via Toll-like receptors (TLR3, TLR7, TLR8) and cytoplasmic sensors (RIG-I, MDA5). This causes translational suppression and mRNA degradation before sufficient protein is produced.

Incorporating N1-methylpseudouridine (m1Ψ) in place of uridine residues dramatically reduces innate immune activation while increasing translational efficiency 10-fold. This pseudouridine modification is the key innovation enabling mRNA therapeutics — developed by Drew Weissman and Katalin Karikó (2005 Nobel Prize in Medicine).

## Spike Glycoprotein and Antigen Design

For COVID-19 vaccines, the antigen is the SARS-CoV-2 spike glycoprotein. The spike undergoes conformational change from prefusion to postfusion upon ACE2 receptor binding. Vaccines encode the prefusion-stabilized form (two proline substitutions K986P/V987P) to maximize neutralizing antibody titers.

The receptor-binding domain (RBD) is the primary target for neutralizing antibodies. Antibody titer (measured by ELISA or PRNT50) correlates with protection against severe disease.

## Immune Response Cascade

After LNP uptake by dendritic cells at the injection site:
1. Endosomal escape releases mRNA into cytoplasm
2. Ribosomes translate spike protein over 24–72 hours
3. Antigen presentation via MHC-I and MHC-II pathways
4. CD8+ cytotoxic T cell activation
5. CD4+ T helper cell activation → Germinal center reaction → B cell affinity maturation
6. IgG antibody production (IgG1, IgG3 subclasses for COVID-19)
7. Long-lived plasma cells and memory B/T cells

## BNT162b2 and mRNA-1273

Pfizer-BioNTech's BNT162b2 uses m1Ψ-modified mRNA encoding prefusion-stabilized spike, encapsulated in ALC-0315/ALC-0159 LNP. Moderna's mRNA-1273 uses SM-102 ionizable lipid. Both achieve ~95% vaccine efficacy against ancestral SARS-CoV-2 strain.

Protein production peaks at 24 hours post-injection and clears within 2 weeks. mRNA does not integrate into host cell DNA (no reverse transcriptase present in human somatic cells).
